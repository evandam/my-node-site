About Me

I have a passion for staying up to date on the latest technology and application development tools and trends.

I think of myself as a very detail-oriented and self-motivated person, and love the challenges that software development brings and the creative thinking it requires. I take a great deal of pride in my work, and always look for elegent solutions to complex problems.

I graduated from Marist College in 2014 with a degree in Computer Science and a minor in Information Technology, with a concentration in Game Design & Programming. I was able to study some very interesting topics, including Artificial Intelligence, Android Game Programming, Computer Graphics, Algorithms, and Operating Systems.

I am currently in the Operations, Technology & Data Leadership Development Program at The Hartford.

So far, I have worked as a technical analyst within the Chief Technology Office, and as an application developer in Personal Lines IT. During school, I also held a summer internship working as a developer in Enterprise Risk Management IT.

As a technical analyst, I learned about the infrastructure of a large enterprise, and how it is managed. This included identifying cost-saving opportunities by retiring and upgrading old hardware and software, and moving towards virtualization.

As a developer, I gained a new perspective as how applications are created and managed in a large company. My role brought me through multiple phases of an IT project, learning how requirements are gathered and implemented into a final product. I was able to get an introduction to new front-end frameworks and build processes such as AngularJS, Grunt, and Bower to manage dependencies.
